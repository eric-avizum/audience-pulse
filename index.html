Executive Summary

Fifty-three pharma CI professionals walked into a room that was already burning. The headline number is 3.2 — the gap between how well this audience detects signals (mean 5.6 out of 7) and how quickly they can act on them (mean 2.4). That is not a rounding error. It is the distance between knowing and doing, and it defines the challenge this masterclass was built to address.

The sensing scores tell a story of genuine capability. On spotting external opportunities (mean 5.5, median 6), this room rates itself well above the midpoint — emerging indications, new modalities, and partnership signals are landing on desks. Detecting fundamental industry shifts (mean 5.5, median 6) is similarly strong: GLP-1 disruption, cell and gene therapy economics, and AI in drug discovery are not invisible to this cohort. Anticipation versus competitors (mean 5.7, median 6) rounds out a sensing triad that sits comfortably in the upper half of the scale.

Then the velocity question hits. At a mean of 2.4 and median of 2, signal-to-action speed collapses. 96.3% of respondents land in the "Stuck at Sensing" quadrant — high awareness, low execution. Only 1.9% qualify as Leaders with both high sensing and high action. The room is not blind. It is paralysed.

The gap is remarkably uniform across segments. By company size, it ranges from 2.94 to 3.42 — neither small firms nor large enterprises escape the execution bottleneck. By role, Other shows the widest gap at 4.22, while Senior Director sits at 2.73. The pattern is structural, not positional. It does not live in one seniority band or one organisation type. It lives in the system.

The data is clear: this room does not have a sensing problem. It has a mobilisation problem. The masterclass question is not "how do we see better" but "how do we move faster once we see."
